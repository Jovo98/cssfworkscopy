Snakes smell with their tongues

By flicking their tongues in the air, they collect scent particles that are then analyzed by an organ in the roof of their mouth called the Jacobson’s organ.

Dolphins have names for each other

They use a unique whistle, referred to as a “signature whistle,” to identify and call each other.

There's a frog that can fly

The Wallace's flying frog - one of the weirdest frogs in the world - glides using its huge webbed feet, which enable it to control its descent and even steer in mid-air.
